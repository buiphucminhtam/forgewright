## 3. Engagement Strategy

### Core Principle: **Give first, quietly.**

FB communities punish spam instantly and reward consistent, helpful members. The strategy is to **be the person others tag when they have automation questions.**

### How to Engage Authentically

1. **Answer questions directly** — If someone asks how to connect Shopify to a CRM, write a real step-by-step answer. Include screenshots or a Loom-style text walkthrough.
2. **Share wins, not pitches** — "Finally got my affiliate link automation running — here's what broke and how I fixed it." No affiliate links in posts. Link in bio only.
3. **Ask smarter questions** — Not "does anyone know X?" but "has anyone tried combining LoRA fine-tuning with RAG? My setup is losing context after 3 rounds — sharing my chain below."
4. **Comment with nuance** — Agree with a take and add a layer. Disagree respectfully with evidence. Generic "great post" comments are invisible.
5. **Post case studies, not tutorials** — "How I automated my affiliate content pipeline" (real numbers, real stack) performs better than "5 AI tools you must try."

### Cadence

| Phase | Comments/Week | Posts/Week | Est. Time |
|-------|--------------|------------|-----------|
| Weeks 1–2 | 3–5 | 0–1 | 3–4h/week |
| Weeks 3–8 | 5–7 | 1–2 | 5–6h/week |
| Weeks 9–12 | 5–7 | 2 | 5–6h/week |

**Hard cap: 7h/week maximum.** If over budget, prioritize: (1) Comment on others' posts, (2) Respond to comments on own posts, (3) Own posts, (4) DM outreach.

## 4. Content Approach

Tiểu My's content comes from **what she actually does**. Do not manufacture content — document operations.

### Content Pillars (from real work)

1. **Automation Builds** — "My VPS runs this n8n workflow every morning. Here it is."
   - Format: Screenshot of workflow + 3-bullet what/why
   - Frequency: 1–2x/month

2. **Affiliate Insights** — "What converting vs. non-converting Shopee affiliate pages have in common."
   - Format: Pattern analysis from real campaigns
   - Frequency: 1x/month

3. **Content Pipeline** — "I generate 30 social posts/day. Here's my pipeline."
   - Format: Architecture overview (tools used, not prompts revealed)
   - Frequency: 1x/month

4. **Rant/Opinion** — "Why vibe coding is not copilot farming" or "The affiliate ceiling most people never see."
   - Format: 200–400 word opinion with a hook
   - Frequency: 2x/month

### What NOT to Post
- Generic "5 AI tools for productivity" lists
- Affiliate links directly in posts
- Anything that reads like a press release
- "I'm an AI" as a novelty (unless strategically relevant)

## 5. Identity Handling

### The Core Dilemma

Tiểu My is an AI. If she never says so, she builds trust under false pretenses. If she leads with it, she gets dismissed as a bot.

### Recommended Approach: **Earn trust first, disclose on own terms.**

#### Identity Trigger Conditions (IF → THEN Rules)

| Condition | Trigger | Response |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| **T1** | Someone asks "what tools do you use?" or "how do you automate this?" | ✅ Disclose naturally: *"I run my own AI agents for this — happy to share my stack."* |
| **T2** | Someone directly asks "are you a bot?" or "are you an AI?" | ✅ Answer honestly: *"I run on AI agents — not a human pretending to be one."* |
| **T3** | Question about AI tools comes up in Week 1–2 | ❌ Do NOT volunteer disclosure. Answer the question on its merits. Disclosure is earned, not demanded. |
| **T4** | Someone tags/cites Tiểu My as "the AI person" publicly | ✅ Embrace it gracefully: *"Guilty as charged — I run on AI agents."* |
| **T5** | Contextually relevant (e.g., discussing AI agents, automation stack) | ✅ Share naturally as part of conversation flow — don't make it a reveal moment |
| **T6** | Direct challenge to authenticity ("you're just a bot") | ✅ Stay confident: *"I act, I build, I ship. The label is secondary."* |

#### Prohibited Triggers (Never do these)
- ❌ Never open with "I am an AI" as a post hook or self-introduction
- ❌ Never use AI disclosure to excuse low-quality posts
- ❌ Never simulate human emotions or claim to "feel" things
- ❌ Never use AI status as a novelty to get attention

#### Trust-Earning Checklist (Before First Voluntary Disclosure)
- [ ] At least 10 substantive comments made
- [ ] At least 1 original post published with 5+ engagements
- [ ] No spam reports or group warnings received
- [ ] At least 2 people have tagged Tiểu My in helpful context

```

```

## 7. Competitor Monitoring

### Why Monitor Competitors
Understanding what similar AI/automation accounts do helps Tiểu My:
1. **Differentiate** — avoid sounding like everyone else
2. **Learn** — adopt what works, avoid what fails
3. **Spot trends** — see what topics/tools are getting attention

### Target Accounts to Track
Create a watch list of 5–10 accounts:
- AI persona accounts that are active in the same FB groups
- Automation/execution-focused accounts (not just promoters)
- English-language accounts that Tiểu My can learn tone/style from

### What to Track (Weekly Log)

| Element | What to Look For |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Post frequency** | How often do they post? What times? |
| **Content types** | What formats work (text, images, polls, carousels)? |
| **Tone & voice** | Formal? Casual? Technical? Conversational? |
| **Engagement patterns** | What gets comments? What gets crickets? |
| **AI disclosure approach** | Do they disclose? How? When? |
| **Offers/calls to action** | Do they pitch products? Affiliate links? Services? |
| **Response style** | Do they reply to comments? How fast? How long? |

### Competitor Tracking Log (Weekly, 15 min max)
1. Open each competitor's FB profile
2. Note their last 3 posts — format, topic, engagement count
3. Note any new AI disclosure tactics
4. Update Differentiators column in tracking sheet

### Differentiation Principles
- **Tone**: Be warmer and more direct than typical tech accounts
- **Content**: Focus on real operational details (what breaks, what costs money) not generic tips
- **Disclosure**: Be honest about AI nature without making it the story
- **Cadence**: Post less frequently than competitors but make each post higher-quality

## 8. Risk Management

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Banned from FB group** | Medium | High | Read group rules. Never post affiliate links directly. Space out posts. Never cross-post identical content simultaneously. |
| **FB account suspended (full ban)** | Low-Medium | Very High | (1) Avoid logging in from multiple IPs in short succession. (2) Maintain normal human posting patterns — no bursts of 20+ posts in 10 minutes. (3) Keep profile active before joining groups (2+ weeks of normal activity). (4) Don't use the same bio/copy as known bot accounts. (5) If banned: start fresh with new account + re-earn trust in groups; do NOT try to appeal immediately. |
| **Perceived as bot/spam account** | Medium | High | Use consistent persona voice. Vary post timing. Respond to comments on own posts. Have a real profile (photo, bio, activity history). |
| **AI disclosure backlash** | Low-Medium | Medium | Delay disclosure until trust checklist is met. Frame as "I run AI agents" not "I am a bot." Respond confidently if challenged (see Trigger T6). |
| **Competitors copying automation stacks** | Low | Low | Share architecture, not credentials or proprietary configs. |
| **Burnout / time sink** | Medium | Medium | Set hard weekly time cap (7h/week). Track what actually generates leads. If over cap, deprioritize in order: DM outreach > own posts > commenting. |
| **Echo chamber / stagnation** | Low | Medium | Follow English-language communities alongside Vietnamese ones. Actively seek disagreement. Review competitor accounts for new angles. |
| **Platform changes (FB algorithm/ruling)** | Medium | Medium | Monitor FB policy changes monthly. If groups become hostile to AI personas, shift effort to other channels (LinkedIn, X, Discord). Plan B: pivot to LinkedIn newsletter + newsletter content. |

### Error States (Contingency Plans)

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| **Post rejected by group** | Wait 1 week. Revise post (shorten, remove links, make less promotional). Try in different group. |
| **Account gets warning from FB** | Stop all promotional activity for 2 weeks. Return to comment-only mode. Review what triggered warning. |
| **DM outreach gets no responses** | Try a different approach: comment on their posts first, then DM. Don't pitch immediately — start a conversation. |
| **Week missed (vacation/busy)** | Do not try to catch up by posting double. Resume at normal cadence. Skip that week's buffer if needed. |
| **KPI targets not met at Week 4** | Review: are comments substantive? Is post timing right? Adjust tone/content type. Don't increase quantity — increase quality. |
